Dietary Tips For Hypothyroid


Iodine - Rich Foods

Incorporate iodine sources like iodized salt, seaweed, fish, dairy, and eggs into your diet, as iodine is essential for thyroid hormone production.

Selenium

Include selenium-rich foods like Brazil nuts, seafood, poultry, eggs, and sunflower seeds. Selenium helps in converting thyroid hormones into their active form.

Gluten-free Options

Some people with hypothyroidism find that a gluten-free diet can help alleviate symptoms. Consider trying gluten-free grains like quinoa, rice, and buckwheat.

Limit Goitrogenic Foods

Goitrogens can interfere with thyroid function. Foods like cabbage, broccoli, cauliflower, kale, and soy should be eaten in moderation, especially if raw.

Balanced Diet

Focus on a balanced diet with whole foods, including lean proteins, healthy fats (like avocados, nuts, and olive oil), and complex carbohydrates (such as whole grains, fruits, and vegetables).

Monitor Soy Intake

Soy contains compounds that can interfere with thyroid hormone absorption. Limit soy consumption and opt for fermented soy products like tempeh and miso.

Limit Processed Foods

Reduce intake of processed foods, refined sugars, and excessive caffeine as they can negatively impact thyroid function and overall health.





Dietary Tips For Hyperthyroid


Moderate Iodine Intake

While iodine is essential for thyroid function, excessive amounts can exacerbate hyperthyroidism. Avoid high-iodine foods like seaweed, iodized salt, seafood, and dairy products.

Reduce Stimulants

Limit caffeine intake as it can stimulate the thyroid gland and worsen symptoms. This includes coffee, tea, and certain energy drinks.

Limit Goitrogenic Foods

Similar to hypothyroidism, minimize goitrogenic foods like broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, kale, and soy products, as they can interfere with thyroid function.

Balanced Diet

Emphasize a well-balanced diet with a variety of nutrient-dense foods. Focus on lean proteins, healthy fats, and complex carbohydrates from whole grains, fruits, and vegetables.

Avoid Excessive Iodine Supplements

Refrain from taking iodine supplements unless specifically advised by a healthcare professional. Excess iodine can aggravate hyperthyroidism.

Consider Calcium & Vitamin D

Some medications for hyperthyroidism can affect bone health. Ensure adequate calcium and vitamin D intake through dairy products, leafy greens, fortified foods, and supplements if needed.

Frequent, Smaller Meals

Eating smaller, more frequent meals throughout the day can help stabilize energy levels and prevent spikes in metabolism.
